## Phase 3 — Three Menu Options

### Option A — Add full footer nav to landing page only

Add Privacy, Terms, and Sign In to the landing page footer. Targeted fix — only the most-visited public page.

```
Footer: Privacy | Terms | Sign In
```

- Fixes the landing page gap.
- Leaves /r/[slug] with no footer.
- Minimal change surface.

---

### Option B — Add minimal legal footer to landing page only

Add Privacy and Terms to the landing page footer only. No Sign In (already in the header CTA).

```
Footer: Privacy | Terms
```

- Clean, legal-only footer.
- Still leaves /r/[slug] bare.

---

### Option C — Normalize all public page footers (Recommended)

Standardize all public surfaces to the same footer pattern: Home | Privacy | Terms.
Apply to: landing page (`/`) and shared recipe page (`/r/[slug]`).
The `/privacy` and `/terms` pages already have their own footer — no change needed there.

```
/ footer:         Privacy | Terms
/r/[slug] footer: Home | Privacy | Terms
```

- Every public surface now has legal discoverability.
- Consistent footer signature across the brand.
- Shared recipe pages often reach new non-users — legal footer is important there.

---

## Phase 4 — Recommendation

**Option C applied.**

Rationale: The shared recipe page (`/r/[slug]`) is the primary public-facing surface for IngredientBot — it's the page that gets shared on social media and reaches non-users. Leaving it with zero footer coverage is a legal and brand gap. The landing page also lacked legal links. Option C closes both with a consistent, minimal pattern (Home | Privacy | Terms) that matches what `/privacy` and `/terms` already use. Option A or B would have left the share page uncovered.
